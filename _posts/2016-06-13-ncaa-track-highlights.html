2016 NCAA Outdoor Track & Field Championship highlights

By Rob Denton | The Register-Guard
June 13, 2016

The Ducks did not win a team title this year but the NCAA championships were still exciting. Many records were broken, including one from 1936 and another from 1966, and Oregon’s two-sport star returned to the national stage.

Here are some highlights from the 2016 NCAA Outdoor Track & Field championships. For more, check out our Track & Field section.

Edward Cheserek
Oregon

Cheserek tied the NCAA record for most titles by winning the 10,000 Wednesday and the 5,000 on Friday. The Oregon junior is the first to win those two events back-to-back since Suleiman Nyambui recorded three-straight distance sweeps between 1980-82.

Ariana Washington
Oregon

Redshirt freshman Ariana Washington, a hotshot recruit in 2015 who sat out her first season at Oregon with a foot injury, blew up the form charts with surprise victories in the 100 and 200, the first individual to win both sprints at the NCAA meet since South Carolina’s Aleen Bailey in 2003.

Jarrion Lawson
Arkansas

The Arkansas senior became the first athlete to win titles in the long jump, 100 and 200 in one meet since Jesse Owens in 1936. The 31½ points Lawson contributed to his team were the most since Owens scored 40 for Ohio State 80 years ago.

Donavan Brazier
Texas A&M

The Texas A&M freshman won the 800 in 1 minute, 43.55 seconds to break the collegiate record of 1:44.3 set by Kansas legend Jim Ryun on June 10, 1966.

Dominique Scott
Arkansas

Arkansas' ace, Dominique Scott, followed up her victory in the 10,000 on Thursday with a meet-clinching triumph in the 5,000 to give her team the title.

Raevyn Rogers
Oregon

Sophomore Raevyn Rogers lived up to expectations with her repeat performance in the 800, making her the first to win back-to-back titles since Geena Gall won in 2008 and 2009 for Michigan.

Courtney Frerichs
New Mexico

The entire steeplechase field took a backseat to Courtney Frerichs of New Mexico, who set a collegiate record by running 9:24.41 to break Jenny Simpson’s collegiate record of 9:25.54, set in 2009.

Devon Allen
Oregon

Allen’s win in the 110 hurdles capped his return from a knee injury that stole his 2015 season. The 2014 NCAA and U.S. champion won convincingly in 13.50 for his second national title in as many attempts.
