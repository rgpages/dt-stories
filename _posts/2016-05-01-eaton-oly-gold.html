An event-by-event look at how Ashton Eaton won his second Olympic decathlon gold medal at the 2016 Rio games

By The Register-Guard
May 1, 2016

Ashton Eaton won his second Olympic gold medal in the decathlon Thursday, tying the Olympic record of 8,893 points set by Roman Szeberle of the Czech Republic.

Eaton was only up by 44 points going into the final event but ran a 4:23.33 1,500 meter, besting second-place Kevin Mayer of France by 2 seconds and securing his victory.

Below you will find a breakdown of how Eaton faired in each decathlon event and there is plenty of additional Olympic coverage at registerguard.com/sports.
