The first few days of the Trials have been filled with fireworks, including some record performances.

Here are some highlights from what we’ve seen at Hayward Field so far. As always, there are a lot of great stories and photos in our Track & Field section.

English Gardner
Women’s 100m

Six Ducks made the women’s 100-meter semifinals, three made the final, one is an Olympian. Sprinter English Gardner is headed to Rio in August, earning her first spot in the Summer Games with a blazing performance Sunday. Read more about the race here and about the person here.

Ashton Eaton
Men’s decathlon

Eaton didn’t set a world record this weekend, but he did send a message. The Olympic champ, competing at less than 100 percent physically, is still considerably better than everybody else. He won his fourth U.S. title on Sunday, scoring 8,750 points to qualify for his second Summer Games. Read more about this year’s performance here and about his record-setting 2012 performance here.

Allyson Felix
Women’s 400m

Felix moved from fifth to first in the final 100 meters to finish in 49.68 seconds, the fastest time in the world since she ran 49.26 to win the World Championships last year. It was the third-fastest time of her career under adversity that had her coach proclaiming it Felix’s best moment. Former Oregon star Phyllis Francis was second with a personal best of 49.94, and Natasha Hastings grabbed the final Olympic spot in 50.17. Read more here.

Galen Rupp
Men’s 10,000m

Rupp’s eighth career 10,000 victory secured his chance to double at the Summer Games in Rio and made him the first American to win eight straight titles in any event. Venerable Bernard Lagat, 2015 World Outdoor Championships qualifier Hassan Mead, former Duck Eric Jenkins and 2016 NCAA runner-up Futsum Zienasellassie all did not finish the race. Read more here.

 
Women’s high jump

Heading to Rio in the women’s high jump are a three-time Olympian and mother of three, a teenage world champion and daughter of an NFL quarterback, and an athlete returning from a doping suspension, festooned with more than 30 tattoos and wearing a two-piece red outfit topped off by a blue Mohawk haircut. They are, respectively, 32-year-old Chaunté Lowe, 18-year-old Vashti Cunningham and 29-year-old Inika McPherson. Read more here.

Brittney Reese
Women’s long jump

On Reese’s fourth attempt Saturday, she thrilled 21,866 fans at Hayward Field when her soaring leap measured 23 feet, 11¾ inches for the win and more important, wind-legal at 1.7 meters per second on a day in which many jumps exceeded the allowable 2.0. In the process, Reese set or equaled several records and all-time accomplishments in the women’s long jump. Read more here.

Alysia Montano
Women’s 800m

The women’s 800-meter final was marred by a collision coming out of the final curve and form chart favorite Alysia Montano fell to the track. She eventually finished the race, nearly a minute after her fellow competitors, to cheers from the crowd. Kate Grace won the 800-meter final in 1:59.10 in one of the more shocking victories of these Olympics Trials. The 25-year-old Yale graduate was injured last season. Read more about the race here and about Montano here.
